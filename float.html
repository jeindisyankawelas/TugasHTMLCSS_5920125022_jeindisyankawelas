"Life is like riding a bicycle. To keep your balance you must keep moving." - Albert Einstein
In 1817 Baron von Drais invented a walking machine that would help him ged around the royal gardens faster.two same-size in-line wheels, the front one steerable, mmounted in a frame upon which you straddled. The device was proppelled by pushing you feet agalnst the ground, thus rolling yourself and the device forward in a sort of gliding walk.

The machine became known as the Draisienne (or "hobby horse"). it was made entirely or wood. This enjoyed a short lived popularity as a fad. not being practical for transportation any other place than a well maintained pathway such as in a park or garden.

The next appearance of a two-wheeled riding machine was in 1865, when padals were appelied directly to the fron wheel. Theis machine was known as the velocipede (meaning"fast food") as well as the "bone shaker," since it's wooden structure combined with the cobblestone roads of the day made for an extremely uncomfortable ride, They also became a fad and indoor riding academies, similar to roller rinks, could be Found in large cities.
